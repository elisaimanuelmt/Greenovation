Workshop at SDN Manyar Sabrangan, Surabaya

On October 4, 2024, Greenovation held an educational workshop at SDN Manyar Sabrangan, Surabaya. The workshop aimed to educate students on the importance of managing organic waste, especially water hyacinth, and to introduce its potential to be transformed into valuable products.

The workshop began with a presentation on environmental themes, energy conservation, and the positive impacts of water hyacinth processing. We provided an in-depth explanation of the importance of protecting the environment and how simple daily actions can contribute to sustainability. The material was designed to give students clear and practical insights into relevant environmental issues.

The event began with an introduction by the Greenovation team, warmly welcomed by the students and teachers. The first session was an interactive presentation on the negative impact of uncontrolled water hyacinth growth on the environment and its potential as a resource for business opportunities. Students were encouraged to think critically about how waste, which often seems useless, could be turned into valuable products like recycled paper.

After the presentation, the students engaged in a hands-on activity to make recycled paper from water hyacinth. They followed each step of the process â€” from crushing the water hyacinth into pulp, pouring it into molds, and drying it to produce sheets of paper. This part of the workshop captured the students' attention, as many of them were experiencing this process for the first time. Some students encountered difficulties in spreading the pulp evenly in the molds, but with guidance from the Greenovation team, they managed to produce paper sheets successfully.

To keep the students energized, a short ice-breaking game was conducted in the middle of the session. However, managing the students' excitement during the game proved to be a challenge. To address this, the Greenovation team decided to switch to a more structured and engaging activity that kept the students focused and actively involved.

The workshop concluded with a reflection session and a discussion on entrepreneurship. The Greenovation team introduced the students to the idea of turning recycled paper into marketable products, such as eco-friendly paper for crafts or notebooks. The students showed great enthusiasm as they imagined creating and selling their own products. The teachers also appreciated the initiative, as it provided the students with not only technical skills but also an early introduction to entrepreneurial thinking.

Through this workshop, Greenovation hopes to inspire students to become more environmentally conscious and foster an entrepreneurial spirit from a young age. By equipping them with waste management skills and business insights, the goal is to nurture a generation that actively contributes to sustainable environmental and economic solutions.
