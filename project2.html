Collaboration with Water Hyacinth Craftsmen (Witrove)

On September 2nd, 2024, we had the privilege of visiting Witrove, a company specializing in transforming water hyacinth into eco-friendly products. During our visit, we met Mr. Supardi, the husband of the founder, Mrs. Wiwit, who shared the inspiring story behind their business. Mrs. Wiwit initially attended a 10-day water hyacinth empowerment program in 2007, which sparked the idea of turning this abundant and inexpensive plant into valuable products. Together, they established Witrove with the aim of creating sustainable solutions.

Mr. Supardi explained how the companyâ€™s sales experienced a significant boost after they showcased their products at the Jatim Expo, an event that supports local businesses. Their presence at the expo also garnered media attention from Kompas, a major news outlet, further boosting their visibility. Witrove has effectively leveraged social media and online marketplaces to reach a broader audience, and their products are now sold internationally through platforms like Shopee in Singapore, the Philippines, and Malaysia.

With a profit of 1.5 billion rupiah from their water hyacinth ventures, Witrove has also been invited to international exhibitions in places like Amsterdam and Korea. In addition, the local government has enlisted them to train communities on cultivating and processing water hyacinth, furthering their positive impact on both the local economy and the environment. The success of Witrove reflects the dedication and collaboration of Mrs. Wiwit and Mr. Supardi, whose efforts have taken their business to new heights both locally and globally.
